About the Menachery-Erinjery Family

The Menacherys or Menasserys, a large 'clan', are spread across the former Cochin State, Kerala, and beyond. This group shares a name and characteristics, with subgroups like Menachery-Erinjery, Menachery-Irumpen, and Menachery-Myliparamban. Clusters of Menacherys can be found in Njarakkal, Parur, Angamaly, Karimalur, Chalakkudy, Mukkattukara, Ollur, Cherpu, and Chiyyaram.

Sub-groups

The Menachery-Erinjery sub-groups of Ollur, Cherpu, and Chiyyaram were once part of the Pazhuvil parish. Their connection to the Kattur-Pazhuvil coastal belt and the Mukkatukara sub-group warrants further research. The possibility of their roots tracing back to the 'Pudumenassery' family, who accepted the faith from the Apostle, is another area of interest.

Connection to Apostle Thomas

Family history strongly suggests a link to Apostle Thomas. Notably, a Menassery hamlet exists near Kokkamangalam, where the Apostle preached. A church and a temple in the area further support this claim.

The Menacherys trace their expansion from Njarakkal near Menassery and Pallipuram to the Cherpu-Ollur-Chiyyaram area. They boast a history of eminent Malpans and priests, with three brothers joining the Order of Discalced Carmelites (OCD) in Vazhakkulam in the 19th century.
